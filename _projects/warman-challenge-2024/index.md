## Overview

This university project involved designing a robotic system for the 2024 Warman Challenge to autonomously retrieve and dispose of six seed pods into a designated incinerator. The project focused on precise actuation, payload handling, and system integration under time and resource constraints.

## Compact Lightweight Actuators

I led the design and fabrication of a custom stacked planetary gearbox with a 16:1 ratio. The actuator was built to provide high torque and low backlash in a compact form, allowing smooth and accurate payload manipulation across the robotic arm's range of motion.

## Electronics System

The full electronic system was wired using diagrams produced in Fritzing. The wiring work focused on clean cable management and reliable integration between the control electronics, motors, power distribution, and support hardware.

## Challenges

Key challenges included balancing torque and precision while managing layout constraints in a tight form factor. Iterative gearbox and motor selection, along with careful electronics integration, supported robust and consistent performance during testing.

## Outcome

This project strengthened my skills in actuator design, motor control, electronics integration, and team-based problem solving under pressure.
