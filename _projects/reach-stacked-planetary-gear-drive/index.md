## Overview

This personal REACH robot project involved designing and testing a stacked planetary gearbox as part of a 6-DOF robotic arm. The design aimed to deliver high torque and low backlash in a compact, 3D-printable form suitable for precise robotic actuation.

## Technical Work

- Designed the stacked planetary gear drive as a compact robotic actuator.
- Produced and assembled 3D-printed components for functional testing.
- Conducted torque testing using a force meter.
- Investigated performance limitations caused by current limits, restricted power supply capacity, and aging electronics.
- Resolved a slipping sun gear issue by redesigning the gear and adding a slip screw.

## Results

Testing showed approximately 41-50% efficiency, which was acceptable for the 3D-printed components used. The gearbox achieved precise motion with minimal backlash, validating the design direction for future robotic arm development.

## Conclusion

This project demonstrated gearbox optimisation, additive manufacturing, and test-based refinement for robotic applications. The results informed future improvements in motor driver capacity, mechanical robustness, and actuator integration.
