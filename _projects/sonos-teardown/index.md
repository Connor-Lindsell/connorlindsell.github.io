## Overview

This project documents the teardown of a Sonos Era speaker to understand how the product is packaged, assembled, and engineered as a compact audio system. The work focused on opening the enclosure, identifying major mechanical and electronic subsystems, and recording design details that would be useful for engineering analysis.

## My Role

I carried out the teardown documentation process, photographed the internal layout, identified key components, and converted the observations into a concise technical reference.

## Technical Work Completed

- Disassembled the speaker while preserving the order of components and assembly features.
- Documented the internal speaker driver arrangement, enclosure structure, and major fastener or retention methods.
- Inspected PCB layout and visible electronic assemblies for engineering reference.
- Recorded observations about manufacturability, serviceability, packaging density, and design tradeoffs.

## Outcomes

The teardown produced a practical reference for how a commercial audio product packages mechanical, electrical, and acoustic subsystems inside a compact enclosure. The page will be expanded with more labelled images and component notes as the original portfolio material is transferred into the website.
